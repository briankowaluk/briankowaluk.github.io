Key Leadership Competencies (KLC)

KLC 1

Create Vision and Strategy

Since December 2021 (2.25 years), I have been the Unit Head (EC-06) and Acting Section Chief (EC-07) for the Innovation Secretariat in the Health Statistics Branch. The mandate of this team is to drive innovation across the various activities of Statistics Canada's health statistics program. There are several ways in which I try to demonstrate this behavior on a daily basis.

First, I create a vision and strategy for this team by reviewing organizational strategic documents, actively listening during meetings with senior management, and consulting with my peers in order to identify key priorities and challenges they face. For example, I have heard from senior management and colleagues that survey response rates have decreased and will not likely recover, so new sources of data will need to be identified in order to measure various population health concepts. They also indicated that collection costs for traditional data sources were increasing, highlighting the need for cost-effective alternatives.

Because of this information, one of the main actions my team takes is to identify alternative sources of health data and test them for relevance to the population health program through a series of assessments. For example, a gap in nutrition data was identified, and my team identified transaction data from grocery retailers as a potential source to fill this need. My team set up a proof of concept project to analyze the transaction data to see if it could produce indicators and trends about nutrition that were relevant. Through the envisioning phase to the completion of the proof of concept, I held numerous meetings with stakeholders to communicate the objectives, potential benefits, project plan, and results. The proof of concept successfully created indicators of nutrition.

My team and I have used this approach with new technologies and in several other health domains. As a result, we have gained a significant amount of knowledge of the policies, applications, and technical implementation considerations and have provided advice to the Director, Director General, and Assistant Chief Statistician on related issues, as well as provided input to various strategies like the Oral Health Statistics Program Treasury Board Submission and the Food Policy Renewal of Canada.

KLC 2

Mobilize People and Leadership

Since December 2021 (2.25 years), I have been the Unit Head (EC-06) and Acting Section Chief (EC-07) for the Innovation Secretariat in the Health Statistics Branch. In this role there are several examples of daily activities that demonstrate my competency to mobilize people and provide leadership.

For example, in order to create a sense of common purpose and direction amongst my team, I maintain an active collaborative space in MS Teams. I use MS Teams to store active documents so that they can be shared easily and worked on collaboratively. I also maintain a task board displayed to the whole team, showing when certain tasks are due, new tasks, how they fit into work priorities, and who is assigned to each task. This has the benefit of showing the team what everyone is working on, allows for linkages to be made across projects, and identifies areas to provide support while waiting on dependencies.

I also maintain weekly one-on-one and team meetings to discuss projects, communicate goals and expectations, address management or administrative needs, share information, and discuss learning and performance. From time to time, I organize a "jam session" — one team member presents the status of a project or findings from analysis to the rest of the team, and in a structured way, others provide input by challenging or building on certain ideas. This allows everyone to bring their different expertise and background to each project, giving them a sense of ownership and a common vision for the outcome.

These daily activities are aimed at ensuring that the team is operating with the same information and aware of the next steps for each project, so they can proactively perform tasks to meet project objectives and can switch between projects and priorities with little friction as needs arise. As a result, the team has completed and contributed to numerous projects delivering on branch, agency, and Government of Canada priorities — steadily taking on more responsibilities, building foundational assets, and building credibility with stakeholders.

KLC 5

Achieve Results

Since December 2021 (2.25 years), I have been the Unit Head (EC-06) and Acting Section Chief (EC-07) for the Innovation Secretariat in the Health Statistics Branch. In my role as Section Chief, an ongoing example of this behavior is our recent effort to maximize dissemination activities for population health statistical outputs.

To ensure the success of this initiative, I adopted a systematic approach focused on being evidence-driven and minimizing costs. Firstly, we conducted an assessment of our existing dissemination activities, identifying bottlenecks, inefficiencies, and areas for improvement. We then identified Statistics Canada web analytics data as a source and collaborated closely with cross-functional teams — including innovation and data analysts, communications staff, and survey program managers — to analyze the data and develop a targeted improvement plan. Together, we set ambitious goals to identify the factors that drive web-based engagement with population health statistical outputs.

By leveraging statistical analysis techniques, we identified patterns, trends, and potential barriers. For example, we noticed that Daily articles published on Wednesdays tended to have the most engagement. We also identified March and November as the months where publishing would result in the most online engagement with statistical outputs. We shared this information with communications staff and survey program managers so that they could develop tailored communication strategies to encourage engagement.

As a result of these efforts, we received positive feedback from stakeholders about the results being actionable and constructive. By consistently demonstrating the ability to achieve tangible results, I contribute to the advancement of our organization's mission and objectives in the field of health statistics.
